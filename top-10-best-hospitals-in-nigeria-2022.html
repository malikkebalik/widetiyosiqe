1. University College Hospital (UCP) – Ibadan

University College Hospital located in Ibadan is the best hospital in Nigeria. It is a federal teaching hospital attached to the University of Ibadan in Ibadan, Oyo state.

The hospital was established in the year 1952 and formally commissioned after completion on 20 November 1957.

It is a government-owned hospital with a capacity of 1000, established for teaching and research purposes.

2. Evercare Hospital Lekki, Lagos

Evercare hospital in Nigeria is a private hospital, owned and run by the Evercare group and is the second-best hospital in Nigeria. An Evercare group is a group that consists of highly skilled medical professionals.

In terms of treatment and services, the hospital has done a fantastic job of satisfying its consumers.

The hospital provides care for infants, children, adults, and the elderly, with specialties in heart care, gynecology, orthopedics, and eye care, among other things.

3. Primus International Super Specialty Hospital

Primus International Super Specialty Hospital is one of the best hospitals in Nigeria. It is located in Abuja, Nigeria.

The 120-bed hospital is a cutting-edge medical facility with cutting-edge medical equipment, designed by a team of internationally known medical experts.

Their super specialty services include kidney transplants,  orthopedics, ophthalmology, ENT and cochlear implants, cardiology, pathology, dentistry, general surgery, and so on making it among the top 10 best hospitals in Nigeria.

4. Lagos Island General Hospital

Lagos Island General Hospital is the first general hospital in Nigeria and one of the top 10 best hospitals in Nigeria.

It was established in the year 1893 during the colonial era as a military hospital for the treatment of the British Armed Forces.

The hospital provides a comprehensive range of healthcare services to the local and international communities, including outpatient and inpatient facilities, Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Surgery, and a training center for Doctors, Nurses, Pharmacists, Radiographers, and Technologist.

5. Lagoon Hospital Ikoyi

Lagoon Hospital established in 1986 is dedicated to providing the finest possible patient care while maintaining the highest levels of professionalism and service.

On May 18, 2011, Lagoon Hospitals became the first hospitals in Sub-Saharan Africa to receive Joint Commission International (JCI) certification, the global leader in enhancing health care quality.

Orthopedics & Trauma, General Surgery, Critical Care, Medical & Surgical Oncology, Urology, Cardiology, Obstetrics & Gynecology, and Pediatrics are among their specializations.

6. Eko Hospital Ikeja, Ikoyi, Surulere Lagos

Eko Hospital Ikeja is one of the top 10 best hospitals in Nigeria and also the first private hospital to be listed on the Nigerian Stock Exchange.

It is a private hospital in Ikeja with an outpost in Ikoyi, Central Lagos, Surulere Lagos State with a capacity of 130 beds space.

Its major mission is to provide a wide range of health services, including secondary services for the local community, and regional, and national health services.

7. St Nickolas Hospital Lagos Island

St Nickolas Hospital Lagos Island established in 1958 is a private hospital with a capacity of 50 beds and one of the best hospitals in Nigeria.

The Hospital is a multi-specialist hospital in Nigeria that is the national leader in kidney disease and healthcare. It is also among the top 10 best hospitals in Nigeria.

8. First Consultant Hospital – First Consultant Medical Centre (FCMC)

First Consultant Hospital is located at st Gregory’s College road, Obalende. FCMC has a dedication to local capacity building, education, and collaborative research through the use of contemporary technology.

They provide complete treatment and deliver quality healthcare every day, not only on special occasions, and also prioritize the patient by creating a healthy medical atmosphere.

9. Cedar Crest Medical Ltd, Abuja

Cedar Crest Medical Ltd, Abuja established in the year 2008 is one of the best hospitals in Nigeria. It is a private hospital that has highly skilled and courteous doctors, nurses, imaging scientists, laboratory scientists, and administrative and ancillary staff.

Among the hospital’s specialist services are internal medicine, obstetrics and gynecology, radiography, rheumatology, ear, nose, and throat surgery, dietetics, physiotherapy, and general practice.

10. Nizamiye Hospital, Abuja

Nizamiye Hospital, Abuja provides a transparent, dependable, and hopeful medical service with their qualified employees and cutting-edge technology.

The owner of Nizamiye Hospital is a group of Turkish and Nigerian investors. It is set out with the primary purpose of reproducing medical services gained overseas in Nigeria.

Since 2014, Nizamiye Hospital has provided high-quality health treatment in all medical disciplines in its five-story facility with a covered area of 12.000 m2.
