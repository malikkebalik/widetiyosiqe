Known facts about Jered Barclay

On November 22, 1930, Jered Barclay was born. When he was just three years old, he made his stage debut in a Vaudeville production alongside Judy Garland, Shirley Temple, and Sammy David Jr. At the age of six, he was introduced to the world of radio, and at the age of twelve, he joined the Clyde Beatty Circus.

In the drama film The Man with the Golden Arm from 1955, Barclay made his acting debut in the mid-1950s and played Frank Sinatra’s cellmate. The Otto Preminger-directed movie, which will be added to the National Film Registry in 2020, received three Academy Award nominations.

Having a successful career as a voice actor, Jered Barclay

On television programmes like Alfred Hitchcock Presents, Cheyenne, The Gray Ghost, and others, Barclay made cameo appearances. Later, he made an appearance in the science fiction drama movie War of the Satellites from 1958. The Roger Corman-directed movie, along with Attack of the 50 Foot Woman, was released as a double feature.

Throughout the 1960s, Jered Barclay kept making cameos on a number of Western television shows, including Rawhide, Cheyenne, Bronco, The Dakotas, Hawaiian Eye, Surfside 6, The Case of the Dangerous Robin, Bonanza, and more. Then he made a cameo in the 1962 television movie His Model Wife.

Later moving to New York, Barclay appeared in Zoo Story and The American Dream, two Edward Albee pieces, at the Cherry Lane Theatre in 1962. He appeared on stage in A Patriot for Me and Marat Sade on Broadway after performing in Next Time I’ll Sing to You at the Phoenix Theatre in 1963. (1969). In addition to Rosebloom and Scandalous Memories, he also directed and choreographed Sextet in 1974 at the Bijou Theatre.

Early in the 1980s, Jered Barclay began doing voice acting for animated TV shows. He was first heard on programmes like Trollkins, Richie Rich, Pole Position, Foofur, and others. Additionally, he provided the voices for The Smurfs and other characters in different episodes of The Transformers. He received a lot of accolades for his work on Paddington Bear.

In the 1980s, Barclay turned to coaching actors, working with names like Carter, Johnny Depp, Josh Brolin, Rue McClanahan, and others. Later, he developed into a professional travel photographer, covering numerous continents and nations for 27 publications.
