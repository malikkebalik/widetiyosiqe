How did Collin Morikawa start his Professional Career?

Collin started his amateur career in his undergraduate years. He won the Western Juvenile in 2013. After that, in 2015, he won the Trans-Mississippi Open. In 2016, he won the Sunnehanna Amateur and the Silicon Valley Amateur.

Additionally, he won the Walker Cup once, the Eisenhower Trophy once, and the Arnold Palmer Cup twice (in 2017 and 2018). (2018). In addition, he held the top spot in the World Amateur Golf Championship for three weeks in 2018.

The Wyoming Desert Intercollegiate, Querencia Cabo Collegiate, and Annual Western Intercollegiate were all titles he earned in 2018. He later won the Pac-12 Championship as well as the 2019 The Farms Open. In addition, Troy Merritt of the United States lost to him in the Barracuda Championship, which he won with a total of 47 points (13-7-13-14).

At the 2019 RBC Canadian Open, Collin Morikawa made his professional start and placed fourteenth. In the same year, he placed second in the 3M Open and fourth in the John Deere Classic. Later, he committed to playing on the PGA Tour in 2019–20. Two weeks later, he defeated Troy Merritt by three strokes to win the Barracuda Tournament, his first PGA Tour contest.

After 72 holes in the 2020 Charles Schwab Challenge in June 2020, he was matched for the lead. a three-month break due to the COVID-19 Pandemic, the PGA Tour restarted competition. After missing a very small par putt on the first playoff hole, he was beaten by Daniel Berger.

On August 9, 2020, he captured the PGA Championship, only his second major championship appearance, and it was his first major tournament win. For the lowest final-round total by a PGA Champion, his 64 tied Steve Elkington’s effort from the 1995 PGA Championship.

He won the WGC-Workday Championship on February 28, 2021, at The Concession Golf Club in Bradenton, Florida. In addition, he defeated Billy Horschel, Viktor Hovland, and Brooks Koepka by a margin of three strokes.

He triumphed in the 2021 Open Championship on July 18 at Kent’s Royal St. George’s Golf Club. Additionally, he outshot Jordan Spieth by two shots. Since Bobby Jones in 1926, Collin is the first player to have won two major titles in as few as eight starts. He also made history by becoming the first player to win two championships in his debut.

He finished the Olympic Games in August in a draw for third place. In a seven-man playoff for the bronze title, he was also eliminated.

He completed the European Tour season by winning the DP World Tour Championship in Dubai in November. He also made history by winning the Race to Dubai as the first American.
