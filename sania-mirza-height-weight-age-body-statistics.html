Sania Mirza is an Indian former professional tennis player who has won 6 Grand Slam titles in her career. In the past, she was ranked as India’s No. 1 player by the Women’s Tennis Association and had received her highest ranking of No. 27 in August 2007. During the course of her career, Sania has participated in numerous tournaments such as the Australian Open, Wimbledon, US Open, French Open, DFS Classic, Sunfeast Open, PTT Pattaya Open, BNP Paribas Open, Aegon Classic, BGL Luxembourg Open, Guangzhou International Open, Family Circle Cup, Sparta Prague Open, and many more. She has been honored with several awards such as the Arjuna Award, Padma Bhushan, Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna, and Padma Shri.

    Sania Mirza Facts

  • Sania achieved World Ranking #27 in singles in August 2007, which is her best career ranking.
  • She won her first “Grand Slam Mixed Doubles Title” in 2009, which was an Australian Open. In 2012, she won the French Open in the “Mixed Doubles” category. Her partner was Mahesh Bhupathi.
  • Mirza started playing tennis at the age of 6 and used to receive training from her father in the initial stages of learning tennis.
  • In 2005, Sania was named WTA‘s “Newcomer of the Year”.
  • Sania’s mom Naseema was selected as the manager for the 2-member tennis team for London Olympics 2012.
  • She once believed in 2010 that her career has ended as she had to undergo three surgeries in a row and was not able to lift even a spoon.
  • Sania’s family is surrounded by sports, especially cricket (her father, husband, and others). But, still, she opted for tennis as it is her favorite sport.
  • She had watched the 2001 movie Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham (also known as K3G) almost 30 times.
  • In 2006, Sania was honored with the Padma Shri, the fourth-highest civilian award of the Republic of India. She received the Padma Bhushan in 2016, which is the third-highest civilian award in India.
  • On November 23, 2013, she was appointed as the UN Women‘s Goodwill Ambassador for South Asia.
  • In 2014, Sania was appointed as the brand ambassador of Telangana.
  • She was included in Time magazine’s “100 most influential people in the world” list in 2016.
  • Sania published an autobiography titled Ace Against Odds in July 2016.
  • In January 2022, Sania announced that she would retire from tennis after the 2022 season. She had retired from the singles’ category of tennis in 2013.
  • Until January 2022, she had won $7,030,997 in prize money as per the Women’s Tennis Association. It increased to US$ 7,261,296 until her retirement in February 2023.
  • She retired from professional tennis in February 2023 after playing her last match at the Dubai Tennis Championships 2023 in Dubai. Her professional tennis career was 20 years long.
  • She had lost the last match of her playing career in Dubai which was the first-round women’s doubles match (which she played alongside Madison Keys).
  • In March 2023, Sania gave a lavish farewell party in Hyderabad which was attended by Irfan Pathan, Farah Khan, Yuvraj Singh, A.R. Rahman, Mahesh Babu, Huma Qureshi, Angad Bedi, and Neha Dhupia. Before the farewell party, she played many exhibition matches that day in Hyderabad with Rohan Bopanna, Yuvraj Singh, and Bethanie Mattek-Sands.
  • In April 2023, Sania gifted Nike black shoes and Balenciaga sunglasses worth ₹1.21 lakh to rapper MC Stan who had performed at Sania’s retirement event in March 2023.
