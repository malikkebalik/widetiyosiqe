Overall Meaning

by Crowbar is a song about the power of dreams to transport us away from the everyday struggles of life. The lyrics are written from a first-person perspective, with the singer describing how they have closed their eyes and entered the "dream weaver train." The dream weaver is a metaphorical figure who can take away the singer's worries and leave tomorrow behind. The song emphasizes the importance of imagination and escapism, suggesting that dreams can help us to forget the pain of the present and look forward to a brighter future.


The chorus of the song repeats the phrase "ooh dream weaver," emphasizing the singer's belief in the power of dreams. They believe that the dream weaver can help them to "reach the morning light" and escape to an "astral plane" where they can forget their troubles. The song also mentions the possibility of flying to the "bright side of the moon," which could be interpreted as a reference to the famous Pink Floyd album.


Overall, Dream weaver is a powerful anthem about the importance of dreaming and escaping from reality. The song celebrates the power of the human imagination and suggests that dreams can provide us with a much-needed respite from the challenges of everyday life.
