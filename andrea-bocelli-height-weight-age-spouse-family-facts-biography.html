Andrea Bocelli is an Italian singer, songwriter, and record producer whose angelic voice and tremendous vocal skills have made him one of the best-selling music artists in the world. Many music experts and leading music artists such as Celine Dion have claimed that Andrea Bocelli has the most beautiful voice in the world and it is almost impossible to argue that. He has sold tens of millions of music albums and has managed to achieve success in classical, jazz, and pop genre.

    Andrea Bocelli Facts

  • His parents were advised to abort him as the doctors felt that the child would be born with a disability. However, his mother decided to go against their advice and Bocelli would later call his mother his inspiration for his opposition to abortion.
  • He was born with some eyesight issues and was eventually diagnosed with congenital glaucoma.
  • At the age of 12, he was hit in the eye by a soccer ball while playing as a goalkeeper in a soccer game. It resulted in a brain hemorrhage and doctors decided to use leeches in an attempt to save his sight. However, they failed and he lost his eyesight completely.
  • After his nanny gifted him the record of Franco Corelli, he started showing interest in becoming a tenor. By the age of 7, he was able to recognize the voice of popular singers and often tried to emulate them.
  • At the age of 6, he started taking piano lessons. Later, he took training for saxophone, guitar, drums, trombone, trumpet, and flute.
  • He gave his first concert performance in a small village which was near to the village he was born in.
  • By the age of 14, he had managed to win the first song competition as he performed O sole mio at the Margherita d’Oro in Viareggio.
  • While studying in college, he used to regularly perform at the piano bars in the evenings to earn some extra money. He worked as a court-appointed lawyer after graduating from college.
  • He was discovered by Italian producer and former singer, Caterina Caselli. Caselli has served as Bocelli’s producer and manager throughout his career.
  • He got his breakthrough when Italian singer and musician, Zucchero, had organized auditions for tenors for the demo tape of his single, Miserere, which he was going to send to Italian tenor Luciano Pavarotti. After hearing Bocelli’s tape, Pavarotti advised Zucchero to use Bocelli in the original recording instead of him.
  • He was signed up by Sugar Music label after record label’s president heard him sing Miserere and Nessun Dorma at Zucchero’s birthday party. Bocelli had accompanied the rock star for his European tour in 1993.
  • He has collaborated with his brother to start Bocelli Family label which is an award-winning collection of wines. The wine is made from grapes grown on his family farm.
  • He made his major operatic role debut as he was cast as Rodolfo in a production of La bohème at the Teatro Comunale, Cagliari in February 1998.
  • In April 1999, he made his debut in the United States as he performed at the John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts in Washington, D.C. The next day he was received at the White House by the then U.S. President Bill Clinton.
  • In November 1999, his autobiographical novel titled as La musica del silenzio was released in Italy.
  • He was invited by Queen Elizabeth II to perform at the annual Royal Variety Performance in Birmingham, UK.
  • In recognition of his contributions to live theatre, he was awarded a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame at 7000 Hollywood Boulevard just in front of the Roosevelt Hotel.
  • Just a day after his father’s death, he gave a live performance for Pope John Paul II in Rome. He was urged by his mother to honor his commitment and immediately after the concert, he returned home for the funeral.
  • In recognition of his contribution to music, he was awarded the Grand Officer of the Order of Merit of the Italian Republic in March 2006.
