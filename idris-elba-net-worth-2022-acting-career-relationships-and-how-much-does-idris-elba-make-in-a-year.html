Idris Elba Biography

Idris Elba was born in the London Borough of Hackney on September 6, 1972, to Winston Elba, a Sierra Leonean Creole man who worked at the Ford Dagenham plant, and Eve, a Ghanaian woman. Elba’s parents married in Sierra Leone before moving to London. Elba was raised in Hackney and East Ham, and at Canning Town High School, where he first became involved in acting, he shortened his first name to “Idris.” He attributes his first big break to seeing an advertisement for a play in The Stage; he auditioned and met his first agent while performing in the role. He began assisting an uncle with his wedding DJ business in 1986; within a year, he had started his own DJ company with some of his friends.

Acting Career

Idris Elba began his acting career in 1994, appearing on British television shows such as Crimewatch, Bramwell, Absolutely Fabulous, The Bill, and Family Affairs. Elba relocated to New York City after appearing in Ultraviolet and Dangerfield.
He established himself in the United States with a theatre role in a production of Troilus and Cressida. Following that, Elba landed a supporting role on the show Law & Order before landing one of his most well-known roles in The Wire. Russell “Stringer” Bell’s role from 2002 to 2004 established him as a well-known figure in the United States.

Following The Wire, Elba returned to his roots with a major role in the film Sometimes in April, which is about the Rwandan genocide. He was then cast in The No. 1 Ladies’ Detective Agency, which was shot in Botswana. In 2009, he was cast in a six-episode arc on The Office, and he later appeared in the season finale. In 2009, he was cast as the lead in Luther, a psychological crime drama series. He later received a Golden Globe nomination for his work on the show. Idris Elba appeared in The Big C in 2010. Elba went on to play Charlie in the Netflix comedy series Turn Up Charlie, which was canceled after only one season.
